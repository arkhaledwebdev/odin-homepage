Salah ad-Din

Sultan of Egypt & Syria

About Me

Salah ad-Din was a prominent Muslim military leader and the founder of the Ayyubid dynasty. Born in 1137 or 1138 in Tikrit, Mesopotamia, he is best known for his role in the Crusades, particularly for recapturing Jerusalem from the Crusaders in 1187. His leadership, military prowess, and chivalry earned him respect from both his Muslim followers and his Christian adversaries. Saladin's reign marked a significant period of consolidation and expansion in the Muslim world, and he is celebrated for his efforts to unite the Muslim territories and his just and honorable conduct. He died in 1193 in Damascus, leaving a legacy as one of the most admired figures in both Islamic and Western history.

Battles

Battle of Hattin
1187
Saladin's forces decisively defeated the Crusader armies, leading to the capture of Jerusalem and the weakening of Crusader states in the Holy Land. This pivotal battle marked a significant turning point in the Crusades, showcasing Saladin's military prowess.
